About

I am a PhD student at TU Wien working on mechanistic interpretability and the analysis of linguistic knowledge in LMs. My research focuses on understanding how LMs represent and process linguistic knowledge cross-linguistically.

I am supervised by Terra Blevins and Allan Hanbury.

Background

Prior to starting my PhD in NLP, I worked on projects in linguistic typology at the Russian Academy of Sciences and completed an internship at the Austrian Academy of Sciences, where I developed NLP pipelines for medieval German texts.

Teaching

Since 2023, I have been involved in teaching programming, NLP, and Information Retrieval courses with up to 300 students at TU Wien and University of Klagenfurt.

Languages

Beyond my passion for NLP, computer science, mathematics, and linguistics, I love learning new languages. I am fluent in Albanian, German, Modern Greek, English, and Russian. Currently, I am learning Indonesian. :)

Collaboration

I am always interested in collaborating on projects involving multilingual NLP and mechanistic interpretability, particularly those focused on analysing linguistic knowledge in language models. Feel free to reach out if you'd like to work together!
